Optimizing Personal Choices

A Visual Guide to the Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) Framework

Tired of second-guessing big decisions? This framework transforms complex problems into a clear, data-driven process, helping you choose with confidence by systematically evaluating what truly matters to you.

The 6-Step Path to Your Best Decision

1

Define

Clearly state your problem and goal. Then, list all possible alternatives without judgment. A clear goal prevents confusion later.

2

Identify Criteria

What factors matter to you? List them all, from cost and time to "comfort" or "social impact." These are your personal standards for success.

3

Measure

Gather information for each alternative. Collect objective data (like price) and assign scores to subjective qualities (like "ease of use" on a 1-10 scale).

4

Weight Criteria

Prioritize what's most important by assigning a "weight" to each criterion. The total of all weights should sum to 100%, reflecting your unique values.

5

Calculate Score

For each alternative, multiply its score on a criterion by that criterion's weight. Sum these up to get a final, comprehensive weighted score.

6

Decide

The alternative with the highest score is your best choice. Use this data-driven insight, combined with your judgment, to make a confident decision.

Weighting: The "Points Allocation" Method

Distribute 100 points among your criteria based on their importance. This forces you to make conscious trade-offs and clearly define your priorities.

Cost 30%
Time 20%
Risk 15%
Benefit 25%
Values 10%

Scoring: Quantitative vs. Qualitative

Both objective numbers (Quantitative) and subjective judgments (Qualitative) are vital. The key is to convert both into a consistent scoring scale (e.g., 0-100).

Interactive Sensitivity Analysis

How robust is your choice? Adjust the weight of a key criterion and see how it impacts the final ranking. If the best option changes easily, your decision is sensitive to that priority.

Note: Other weights will adjust proportionally to keep the total at 100%.
